Arcade Eras

Classic of Their Time

Arcade Games as an Entertainment format has gone through many challenges over the years, in current times finding a dedicated arcade is nearly unheard of, normally they are pushed into the dark corner of a pizza parlor or a limited selection cna be found mixed in with the strangely more popular Skee Ball or Basketball machines.

The time of the Arcade cabinet came about in the late 1970s, Atari's PONG was one of the first and well known games, it was a strong representation of the limitations experienced by companies seeking to make video games, miniturization had not come full swing yet so the interface was simple, black and white, and only capable of counting up to 255.

The 1980s saw large advancements in display and computer technology, allowing for mass market production of arcade machines, complete with full color displays. Many classics that are considered to have shaped videogames for generations were created in this time period

The 90s saw a revival of the video arcade due to bringing much more impressive technology to the format, after the crash of 83 not many companies invested in innovation in Arcade cabinets. However the 1990s saw Neo Geo and their 16 bit processors come into the front, bringing new levels of performance to crowd favorites

The home console market had really taken off at this point, with CD and DVD media for consoles, developers no longer had many limits when bringing full arcade action to peoples homes. The solution, develop games for both Console and Arcade, but release the home version months afterward.
