Side by Side

Classic Release Modern Release
  1. Extremely Unfriendly to casual play, Corpse runs, XP loss, large time sinks
  2. Much larger focus on social interaction, groups with multiple people were easy to come by, general community was positive and outgoing.
  3. User Interface was very restrictive and clunky.
  4. Characters were very weak, most classes could not kill a creature without assistance past level 15
  5. Raid encounters were designed around throwing characters at dragons until one of them died.
  1. Much more casual friendly, death is not very punishing, a lot of content can be done within an hour
  2. Fairly difficult to form a full group without someone playing multiple characters, community has become more cloistered
  3. User Interface is much more streamlined and fully customizable
  4. Characters are exceptionally more powerful at any given level, most classes can solo some content.
  5. Raid Encounters are much more tactically impressive, requiring execution on participants part to complete.

In conclusion, while many people who played Everquest back in 1999 yearn for the game that was back then, what I think they are really missing is the social interaction that took place, instead of the hardcore mechanics that have been replaced, the game is still a place where you can find this interaction, but it is a shadow of its former self, with real player face to face being few and far between.
